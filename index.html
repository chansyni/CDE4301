EIM-454: Minimally Invasive Bariatric Surgical Technique

Abstract

Obesity is a rising global health issue, and bariatric surgery is often recommended to patients with severe obesity. However, traditional bariatric procedures such as gastric bypass and sleeve gastrectomy have a low uptake of less than 1%, due to a diminished patient safety perception of the surgery.

The aim of this project is to increase bariatric surgery uptake by developing a minimally invasive alternative, with fewer risks, shorter recovery times, and similar efficacy to current surgical options. This can enhance safety perception, offering patients a more appealing and accessible alternative to traditional bariatric surgery.
